Your Baby's Development

This week, your baby is about the size of a lime and is busy developing vital organs and systems, such as the kidneys and intestines. Tiny tooth buds are forming, which means baby will be ready for those first little grins in just a few months! Additionally, the brain is growing rapidly, and neural connections are being formed even now.

Mom's Symptoms and Tips

  • Nausea: Try eating small, bland meals throughout the day to help alleviate nausea.
  • Fatigue: Make sure to allow yourself extra rest – taking short naps can be refreshing!
  • Mood swings: Practice mindfulness or gentle yoga to help ground your emotions.

Weekly Tip

Take a 10-minute walk each day to boost your mood and energy levels.

Nutrition Focus

Folate is key this week as it helps in the development of the neural tube and reduces the risk of neural tube defects.

Foods to Avoid

  • Raw fish (risk of parasites)
  • Unpasteurized cheese (risk of listeria)
  • Certain types of fish high in mercury (like swordfish)

Hydration Tip

Aim for at least 8-10 cups of fluids a day to keep you hydrated and support your increased blood volume.
