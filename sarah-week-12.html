Sarah, You're 12 Weeks Pregnant!

Only 28 weeks to go!

Your Baby's Size

This week, your little one is the size of a lime.

Baby's Development

Your baby is developing their vocal cords, which will eventually allow them to make sounds. Fingers and toes are becoming more defined, making your little one look even more like a tiny human! The facial features are also continuing to form, and the baby's eyes are moving towards the front of their head.

Mom's Symptoms

  • Fatigue: Take short naps during the day if possible, and prioritize restful activities.
  • Nausea: Try to eat small, frequent meals and avoid strong smells that trigger nausea.
  • Mood Swings: Practice mindfulness or gentle yoga to help balance your emotions and relieve stress.

Weekly Tip

Engage in a calming activity like reading or taking a warm bath to nurture yourself this week.

Support for John

  • John, consider planning a special evening for Sarah to relax and unwind together.
  • Help Sarah by preparing some healthy snacks that are easy to grab throughout her busy days.
  • Learn about fetal development milestones together, so you can share in the excitement of each week.

First-Time Mom Tip

Remember, it's completely normal to feel overwhelmed at times; every pregnancy is unique, and you're doing great!

Affirmation

You are strong, beautiful, and doing an amazing job, Sarah.

Nutritional Focus

Folic Acid; it helps prevent neural tube defects and is crucial for baby's brain development.

Foods to Avoid

  • Raw fish (e.g., sushi) - Risk of parasites and harmful bacteria.
  • Unpasteurized dairy products - Can carry dangerous bacteria.
  • Deli meats - Risk of listeria unless heated properly.

Hydration Tip

Aim to drink at least 10 cups of fluids daily to stay well-hydrated and support your growing baby.
